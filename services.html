Various Types of NGO's

International

  • Care Canada
  • ICRC
  • IFRO
  • MSF
  • Oxfam International
  • World Vision International

INGOs are non-profit organizations that work globally to address humanitarian, environmental, social, and other issues. They partner with various stakeholders, including governments and other NGOs, to achieve their objectives.

Quasi Autonomous

  • Crown Corporation
  • Fine Gael
  • Crown Entites
  • Forestry Commission
  • Goverment sponsored
  • Indonesian Ulema council

Quasi-autonomous non-governmental organization, an organization or agency that receives funding from a government but is able to act independently.

Environmental

  • Global Alliance on Health and Pollution
  • Earth System Governance Project
  • School Strike for Climate
  • Global Green Growth Institute
  • Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change
  • International Union for Conservation of Nature

An ENGO (environmental non-governmental organization) is a non-governmental organization (NGO) in the field of environmentalism. These organizations operate both locally and internationally which makes them play an important role in dealing with different kinds of environmental issues that are happening in the contemporary world.

Donation to charitable organizations has been a vital source of financial support for various social causes, including healthcare, education, and poverty alleviation. Annually, individuals and corporations contribute significant amounts to these organizations to aid in their charitable work. The growth of digital payment systems has further facilitated the donation process, making it easy and convenient for donors to support their preferred causes. The trend towards socially responsible consumerism has also led to an increased awareness of the importance of charitable giving, resulting in more people contributing to donations organizations than ever before.
